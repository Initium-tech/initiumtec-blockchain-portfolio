**Subject:** Donation partnership inquiry — community project supporting the sapo concho

Dear Puerto Rican Crested Toad Conservancy team,

My name is Rafael García. I'm a software developer in Puerto Rico and the
founder of Initiumtec, a small technology studio. I'm writing to ask whether
the Conservancy accepts donations from community fundraising projects, and
whether — with your written permission — we could name you as the beneficiary.

The project: we are building a community cryptocurrency token themed around
the sapo concho, our island's only native toad. Before launching anything
publicly, we want to do this correctly and transparently:

- A fixed percentage of the project's trading-fee revenue would be donated to
  the Conservancy on a quarterly schedule.
- Every donation would be published with its transaction record, so the
  public can verify each one.
- We will not use the Conservancy's name or logo in any material unless you
  approve it in writing, and you could withdraw that permission at any time.
- We are happy to donate in US dollars via whatever channel you prefer
  (we handle any currency conversion on our side).

To be fully transparent: this is a "meme coin" — a community token whose
value is speculative. Our own materials will say plainly that it is not an
investment. What we can promise is that the donation mechanism is real,
public, and auditable, and that we'd be proud for it to bring both funds and
awareness to the sapo concho's recovery.

Would this be something the Conservancy is open to? I'm glad to answer any
questions, share the project materials in advance, or adjust the structure
to whatever protects the Conservancy's interests best.

Thank you for the work you do for this species.

Kind regards,
